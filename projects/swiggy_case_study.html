The Problem

Group orders break at the
moments that matter most

When urban professionals, friend groups, and co-living flatmates order food together, two critical moments collapse — and Swiggy has no native answer.

When food arrives and nobody knows what belongs to whom, and when the bill comes and one person is left paying for everyone — groups need a way to split fairly and track food ownership without disturbing the event flow. But Swiggy offers no native way to do this, forcing users onto WhatsApp, calculators, and UPI after the order is done.

Pre-Order

Coordination overhead, verbal collection, manual consolidation of everyone's choices.

→

Delivery

Food ownership chaos — no item tagging, confusion at arrival over who ordered what.

→

Post-Order

Unfair splitting, reimbursement friction, and complete platform abandonment.

1

Coordination Overhead

The coordinator collects orders verbally or over WhatsApp, manually consolidates, and places the order alone. The mental load falls entirely on one person.

2

No Item Ownership

There is no way to tag which item belongs to which person inside Swiggy. Ownership exists only in people's memory — or not at all.

3

Unfair Equal Splitting

When the bill is split equally, people who ordered less subsidise people who ordered more — creating silent resentment over time.

4

Reimbursement Friction

Collecting money happens entirely outside Swiggy — WhatsApp reminders, UPI requests, follow-up messages. Some delay, some forget, some avoid.

5

Food Ownership Chaos

When food arrives, nobody remembers who ordered what — especially for similar-looking items. Results in someone eating the wrong food or visible arguments.

6

Platform Abandonment

Users are forced to switch to calculators, WhatsApp, and GPay the moment the bill arrives. The transaction Swiggy initiated ends outside Swiggy.

User-Centric Approach

Built for real user personas

Three distinct user groups emerged from pain point analysis — each with unique frustrations and needs around group ordering.

Office Lunch Coordinator

"I want to manage a group food order and settle the bill fairly without manual effort."

  • Create group order and invite colleagues
  • Each item tagged to the person who ordered
  • Choose Dutch billing at checkout

Weekend Friends Group

"I want our group order handled inside Swiggy, so the evening ends without awkward follow-ups."

  • Join shared group order from a friend
  • Option for one person to pay as a gesture
  • Settlement inside Swiggy before confirmation

Co-living Flatmates

"I want a fast, automatic way to split every group order fairly based on what each person ordered."

  • Quickly tag items in shared cart
  • Dutch billing as default
  • Auto-calculates share and notifies
The Solution

Group Order + Split Payment

A seamless in-app experience that lets groups order together from a single restaurant, tag ownership of every item, and split the bill automatically.

Shareable Group Link

Creator generates a group order link. Friends join via WhatsApp, Instagram, or text message and add their own items to a shared cart.

Collaborative Cart

Every item is auto-tagged to the person who added it. The creator sees a unified cart showing everyone's items with clear ownership.

Smart Bill Splitting

Two modes: Dutch Split (pay for what you ordered + proportional tax + equal delivery) or Equal Split. Calculated automatically.

Key Architecture Decisions

  • Payment Architecture (V1): One person (order creator) pays the full bill to Swiggy. Swiggy calculates shares automatically. Reimbursement happens peer-to-peer via GPay, UPI, or cash.
  • Two-Phase Cart Commit: Phase 1 — Member builds personal temporary cart freely. Phase 2 — Member taps 'Add to Group's Cart' committing selection to creator's view.
  • Co-located Design: Designed for groups physically in the same room. Coordination decisions made verbally. Maximum 12 members per session.
Success Metrics

How we'd measure success

A metrics framework designed to validate adoption, engagement, and conversion — from session creation to completed group orders.

🎯 North Star: Weekly Completed Group Order Sessions

Group order sessions per week where a group order is created, all members commit, the creator completes payment, and share notifications are delivered to all members.
