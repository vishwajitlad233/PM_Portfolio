Section 1 — Problem Statement

When urban professionals, friend groups, and co-living flatmates order food together, the experience breaks down at two critical moments — when food arrives and nobody knows what belongs to whom, and when the bill comes and one person is left paying for everyone. Groups need a way to split fairly and track food ownership without disturbing the event flow — but Swiggy offers no native way to do this, forcing users onto WhatsApp, calculators, and UPI after the order is done. This gap directly impacts Swiggy's average order value and repeat group ordering frequency, as post-order friction discourages groups from making Swiggy their default choice for shared meals.

Section 2 — Pain Points

Mapped sequentially across the group ordering experience: pre-order → delivery → post-order.

Pre-Order

Coordination overhead, verbal collection, manual consolidation.

→
Delivery

Food ownership chaos, no item tagging, confusion at arrival.

→
Post-Order

Unfair splitting, reimbursement friction, platform abandonment.

  • Pain Point 1 — Coordination overhead before ordering: Affected Persona: Persona 1 — Office Lunch Coordinator. The coordinator collects everyone's order verbally or over WhatsApp, manually consolidates it, and places the order alone. There is no shared cart or collaborative ordering mechanism inside Swiggy. The mental load falls entirely on one person before the food is even ordered.
  • Pain Point 2 — No item ownership at checkout: Affected Personas: All Personas. When the order is placed, there is no way to tag which item belongs to which person inside Swiggy. Ownership exists only in people's memory — or not at all. This creates the foundation for every downstream confusion.
  • Pain Point 3 — Food ownership chaos at delivery: Affected Personas: Persona 2 — Friends Group, Persona 3 — Co-living Group. When food arrives, nobody remembers with certainty who ordered what — especially for similar-looking items. The result is either someone eating the wrong food silently or a visible argument at the door. Most acute in casual settings where nobody was tracking carefully.
  • Pain Point 4 — Unfair equal splitting: Affected Personas: Persona 1 — Office Coordinator, Persona 3 — Co-living Group. When the bill is split equally, people who ordered less subsidise people who ordered more. In office settings this creates silent resentment. In co-living groups it becomes a recurring pattern that builds frustration over time. There is no item-level dutch billing option inside Swiggy today.
  • Pain Point 5 — Reimbursement friction after the event: Affected Personas: Persona 1 — Office Coordinator, Persona 2 — Friends Group. After one person pays the full bill, collecting money from others happens entirely outside Swiggy — WhatsApp reminders, UPI requests, follow-up messages. Some people delay, some forget, some avoid. The person who paid absorbs both the financial float and the social awkwardness of chasing.
  • Pain Point 6 — Platform abandonment at the billing moment: Affected Personas: All Personas. Because Swiggy has no splitting or tagging capability, users are forced to switch to a calculator, WhatsApp, and GPay the moment the bill arrives. The transaction that Swiggy initiated ends outside Swiggy. This is a direct platform retention failure at the highest-intent moment of the experience.

Section 3 — User Stories

Persona High-Level Story Sub Stories
Persona 1 — Office Lunch Coordinator As an office lunch coordinator, I want to manage a group food order and settle the bill fairly without manual effort, so that I am not burdened with coordination or reimbursement chasing.
  • Create group order and invite colleagues.
  • Each item tagged to the person who ordered it.
  • Choose dutch billing option at checkout.
Persona 2 — Weekend Friends Group As a member of a friends group, I want our group order and bill to be handled inside Swiggy, so that the evening ends without any awkward follow-ups or pending payments the next day.
  • Join shared group order created by a friend.
  • Option for one person to pay the full bill as a gesture.
  • Bill settlement to happen inside Swiggy before order confirmation.
Persona 3 — Co-living Group As a co-living flatmate, I want a fast and automatic way to split every group order fairly based on what each person ordered, so that billing is always accurate and nobody overpays over time.
  • Quickly tag items in shared cart.
  • Dutch billing as default.
  • App automatically calculates share and notifies amount owed.

Section 4A — Scope & Out of Scope

✅ In Scope ❌ Out of Scope
  • "Group Order" entry point on restaurant page
  • Shareable group order link generation
  • Collaborative cart — members add their own items
  • Item tagging (auto-tagged or manual assignment)
  • Three billing options: Dutch, Equal Split, Paid-by-One
  • Automatic share calculation
  • In-app push notification for shares
  • Single payment by order creator to Swiggy
  • Maximum 12 members per session
  • Multi-restaurant ordering within one group order
  • In-app peer-to-peer payment settlement between members
  • Subgroup-level billing logic
  • Group order creation before restaurant selection
  • Swiggy contacts or friends list integrations
  • Scheduled or pre-planned group orders
  • Group order session expiry timer
  • Full bill passing to another member

Section 5 — Functional Requirements

Cluster 1 — Group Order Creation

FR-1.1 — Group Order Entry Point

User Story

As an order creator, I want to see a 'Group Order' option on the restaurant page, so that I can initiate a group order without leaving the restaurant I've already chosen.

Functional Requirement

The system must display a 'Group Order' button on the restaurant page alongside the existing 'Order Now' option. This button must be visible only when the user is logged in to Swiggy.

Acceptance Criteria

GIVEN a logged-in Swiggy user is on any restaurant page. WHEN the page loads. THEN a 'Group Order' button must be displayed alongside the existing 'Order Now' button. AND THEN tapping 'Group Order' must initiate the group order creation flow without navigating away from the restaurant page.

Cluster 4 — Billing Option Selection

FR-4.2 — Dutch Billing Calculation

User Story

As an order creator who selected Dutch billing, I want each member's share to be calculated based only on the items tagged to them, so that everyone pays exactly for what they ordered.

Functional Requirement

When Dutch is selected, the system must calculate each member's share by summing the prices of all items tagged to that member, including proportional taxes and an equal share of the delivery fee.

Acceptance Criteria

GIVEN the order creator has selected Dutch. WHEN the creator views the breakdown. THEN each member's share must reflect the exact sum of item prices tagged to them. AND THEN taxes must be applied proportionally. AND THEN the delivery fee must be divided equally.

Cluster 5 — Checkout & Payment

FR-5.2 — Creator Completes Payment

User Story

As an order creator, I want to pay the full group order bill through my preferred payment method, so that the order is placed immediately without waiting for other members to pay.

Functional Requirement

The system must allow the creator to complete payment for the full group order amount using any of Swiggy's existing payment methods. The payment flow must be identical to Swiggy's existing single-order payment flow with no modifications.

Appendix — Key Architecture Decisions

  • Payment Architecture (V1): One person (order creator) pays the full bill to Swiggy. Swiggy calculates shares automatically. Actual reimbursement happens peer-to-peer outside Swiggy via GPay, UPI, or cash.
  • Two-Phase Cart Commit Mechanic: Phase 1 — Member builds personal temporary cart freely. Phase 2 — Member taps 'Add to Group's Cart' committing selection to creator's view.
  • Co-located Design Principle: Designed for groups physically in the same room. Coordination decisions made verbally.
